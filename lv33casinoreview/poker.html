Poker Hand Rankings – Which Hand Wins?

Understanding poker hand rankings is key to mastering the game. Below is the official ranking of hands in order, from strongest to weakest:

  1. Royal Flush – The ultimate poker hand, consisting of A, K, Q, J, and 10 of the same suit. This is unbeatable.
  2. Straight Flush – Five consecutive cards of the same suit (e.g., 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 of hearts). Only a higher straight flush or a royal flush can beat it.
  3. Four of a Kind – Four cards of the same rank (e.g., four aces). The fifth card, known as the kicker, is used to break ties.
  4. Full House – A combination of three-of-a-kind and a pair (e.g., three kings and two fives). If multiple players have full houses, the one with the higher three-of-a-kind wins.
  5. Flush – Five cards of the same suit, but not in sequence. The highest card determines the winner in case of a tie.
  6. Straight – Five consecutive cards of mixed suits (e.g., 4♠, 5♥, 6♦, 7♣, 8♠). Aces can be high (A-K-Q-J-10) or low (5-4-3-2-A), but not both in the same hand.
  7. Three of a Kind – Three cards of the same rank (e.g., three queens), with the two highest other cards as kickers.
  8. Two Pair – Two different pairs (e.g., two jacks and two sixes). If multiple players have two pairs, the higher pair wins, or the kicker decides in case of a tie.
  9. One Pair – Two cards of the same rank (e.g., two eights). The remaining three cards (kickers) determine the winner if multiple players have the same pair.
  10. High Card – When no other hand is made, the highest card in the hand determines the winner. This is the lowest-ranking poker hand.

Types of Poker Games

An outline of the various types of poker games to play online.


Texas Hold’em

7/////////////32718 /-->In Hold’em, players receive two down cards as their personal hand (hole cards), followed by a round of betting. The game continues with a three-card flop, a fourth board card, and a fifth board card, with betting rounds in between. The player with the best five-card combination wins.


Omaha

Omaha is similar to Hold’em but with four hole cards. Players must use exactly two hole cards and three community cards to form the best five-card hand.


Seven-Card Stud

Seven-card stud is played with two downcards and one upcard dealt before the first betting round, followed by three more upcards and one final downcard, with betting rounds in between. The best five-card poker hand wins.


Hi-Lo Poker Games

Hi-Lo poker games split the pot between the highest and lowest hands. Examples include:


  1. Hi-Lo Omaha
  2. Seven-Card Stud Hi-Lo

  1. High Hand Poker - The best hand wins (e.g., Texas Hold’em, Omaha).
  2. Low Hand Poker - The lowest hand wins (e.g., Razz).
  3. Hi-Lo Split Poker - The pot is divided between the highest and lowest hands.
