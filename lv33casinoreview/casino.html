The Legal Landscape of Gambling in Bangladesh

Gambling has long been embedded in the cultural history of Bangladesh, dating back to the pre-colonial era. Ancient folklore and historical records indicate that games of chance were often played as a form of entertainment.

During British colonial rule, the Public Gambling Act of 1867 was enacted, and it remains in effect today. This law strictly prohibits gambling activities across the country. Even after gaining independence, Bangladesh has continued to enforce this legislation, often conducting high-profile crackdowns, particularly in Dhaka, to combat illegal gambling. These operations highlight the government's commitment to addressing gambling-related issues such as addiction, financial ruin, and crime.

With the rise of modern technology, including smartphones and the internet, enforcing these regulations has become increasingly challenging. While the existing law remains in place, its lack of clarity regarding online gambling creates a legal gray area. This has led to discussions about potential reforms to address digital betting platforms.
